What we Gardanes in yoreday, thedekings, thrim afrain - how the athelings framed ellen! Oft Shield Sheaving, from threats of sheathens, from many maithes, offtew the meadsettle, eyesed the earls. Sithen erst warth feyshaft found, of that he bade for frover, wox under the wolks, thew by worthmind, 

Oh, what we Spear-Danes in days of yore, kings of the tribes, heard of glory - how the princes performed valor! Often Shield, son of Sheaf would, from many troops of enemies - from many bloodlines, deprive them of their mead benches, and strike fear in the earls.
