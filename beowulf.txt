What we, of Gardanes in yoreday - of thedekings, afrain thrim - how those athelings framed ellen! Oft Shield Sheaving, from threats of sheathens, from many maithes, offtew the meadsettle, eyesed the earls. Sithen erst warth feyshaft found, of that he bade for frover, wox under the wolks, thew by worthmind, 

What glory we have heard of the Spear-Danes in days of yore - of the kings of the tribes - how those princes performed valor! Often Shield, son of Sheaf would, from many troops of enemies - from many bloodlines, deprive them of their mead benches, and strike fear in the earls. Since he was first made found destitute, he strove for consolation because of it, grew under the clouds, thrived by honor,
